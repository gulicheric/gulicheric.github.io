LaMelo Ball: Is the Hype Real?¶

LaMelo Ball has been a popular name in the basketball world for the past few years. In 2017, he made a name for himself in high school as a Sophomore, scoring 92 points in one game and being part of one of the best high schoool teams in the country. Every sports broadcast was featuring this game, and soon after, the gym during his games became packed with fans. After high school, he took his game to Australia, becoming NBL Rookie of the Year. After one year, he entered the NBA draft and was selected number one overall by the Charlotte Hornets. LaMelo is the first player to be in the splotlight so young in his life, and there was a lot of hype surrounding him coming into his rookie season. We will see if his extraordinary play of his younger years transfers into the NBA. Some have said that LaMelo is the greatest basketball player to into the league since LeBron James (arguably the best player ever).

The question becomes: Does LaMel Ball deserve the hype? Is he really that good?

To learn more about LaMelo Ball and his career so far, you can read this article: https://populartimelines.com/timeline/LaMelo-Ball

I will create 4 OLS Regression models, each one letting Minutes Played be the independent variable, while the statistics we want to compare will be the independent variables.

From the OLS Regression models, my predictions prove to be correct, along with a new discovery. Since the P-value is 0 for ever statistic (Points, Rebounds, Assists, FG%), which is less than the alpha value of 0.05, the relationship between them is statistically significant.

We learn that the coeffecients are as follows:
Points = 0.5119
Rebounds = 0.1893
Assists = 0.1341
Field Goal Percentage = 0.0132

The coeffecient tells us the positive or negative correlation between the independent and dependent varibale. Since they are all positive, we see that there is a positive for each one. The number of points has the highest correlation, while rebounds and assists have similar results. The field goal percentage is surprising, since I originally thought that minutes played would have no effect on their minutes played. While the coeffecient is small, we still see there is a small positive correlation between minutes played and field goal percentage. My predication may have been true if I were using data of everyone in the league. However, since I am only comparing the best rookie players of the league of every year, we see that the more minutes they play, the more experience they get to be comfortable shooting.

Per 36-Minute¶

First I will create the statistics to be correlated to per 36 minutes. The reason for doing this is that the NBA usually either creates ratings based off per-36 or per-40 minutes. I arbitrarily chose the per-36 minutes statistic to use for my dataframe. Using either one will have no effect on the output. The website that I retrieved the data does not contain the per 36-minute statistics for each player, so I will do the manipulation myself. I will be using the formula provided by https://www.nbastuffer.com/analytics101/per-minute-ratings/#:~:text=Per%2Dminute%20ratings%20are%20calculated,he'd%20play%2036%20minutes. This website shows the formula for per 36-minutes is (36 * statistic)/minutes, so I will use this in my calculations.

Conclusion: Does LaMelo Ball Deserve to Have the Hype?¶

In short: yes.

We saw that in the past, the best players had the worst statistics their rookie season - which makes sense. They are coming into the league for the first time and it takes time to get into the rythm of playing at such a high level. Therefore, I chose to compare LaMelo Ball to the best rookies of the past 30 years. When first looking at how LaMelo Ball compares to these players, we saw that he ranged from low to mediocre in Points, Rebounds per Game, Assists per Game, and Field Goal Percentage. While this may have been the end of the story, we saw that he was in the bottom 20% in minutes played. As we saw from the OLS Regression models, all these categories have a positive correlation with minutes played. This also makes sense - the more time a player has on the court, the more opportunity they have to add to the stat box. I then adjusted all their statistics to evaluate the players on their performance per 36 minutes. This showed a complete different story than the previous plots. In every category, LaMelo Ball is in the top 10, except for rebounds. Even in the rebound category, LaMelo Ball was 12th on the list. This is a very impressive. It is important to remember that the players that I comparing with LaMelo Ball are Rookie of the Year recipients. They were supposedly the best rookie players of that year. The fact that LaMelo Ball is almost in the top 10 of these players is astonishing. For this reason, LaMelo Ball deserves to have the hype.

While LaMell Ball is projected to be one of the best players in the NBA as he progresses through his career based off his rookie statistics alone, there is an imporant detail to note. Basketball is full intangibles, specifically with injuries. Injuries have plagued some of the best players into becoming mediocre ones after the surgery. Being a great player one year will not automatically translate into becoming a superstar.

Even so, LaMelo had a great season this year and it will be fun to see his development and growth in the next few years.
